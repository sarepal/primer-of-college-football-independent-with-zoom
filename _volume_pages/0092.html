{% raw %}44 A PRIMER OF COLLEGE FOOTBALL{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In runs through the other side of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}line the end should get into the inter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ference or push as quickly as possible.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}When his own side is going to kick, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}end should spread out a little wider from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tackle than ordinarily. He should steal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a little on the ball, if possible, in going{% endraw %}
{% raw %}down the field. If the opposing end{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plays in the line for the purpose of trying{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to stop the kick, he should bunt into him{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in passing, going down on the kick. If{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the opposing end is not in the way, he{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should bump into any dangerous player{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who is likely to stop the kick. But the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}end should not attempt this unless he is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}speedy enough to get down the field. Be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sides, his main business is to get down the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}field. The direction of the end should{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be a little towards the side-lines, but con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}verging upon the catchers. The end{% endraw %}
{% raw %}can tell the direction of the kicks, either{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by looking over his shoulder, or watching{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the movements of the opposing backs.{% endraw %}
