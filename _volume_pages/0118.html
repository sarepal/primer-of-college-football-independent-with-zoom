{% raw %}4. Cent re-Rush.—The position of Centre-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rush is comparatively a new one. Until{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a few years ago the middle position in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}line was occupied by a snap-back, whose{% endraw %}
{% raw %}only duty was to put the ball in play.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}After that, he was merely a passenger.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the snap-back the centre-rush has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been evolved by gradual enlargement of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his duties. To-day he is chief of forwards,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}there being no duplicate to his position,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as there is of tackle and guard. Every{% endraw %}
{% raw %}play starts from the centre-rush, and de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pends upon him for a large share of its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}success or failure.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The position is one requiring painstak¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing, conscientious, hard work, admitting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of very little "glory," although the centre{% endraw %}
{% raw %}handles the ball more than any other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}player. That is the reason why few be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ginners choose that position in the line.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"Hard work," they say, "and no glory."{% endraw %}
