{% raw %}POSITION PLAY{% endraw %}
{% raw %}71{% endraw %}
{% raw %}But, like everything else, the position is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}just what the player makes it.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Physically the centre-rush should be a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}large, strong, and heavy man, who is at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}same time active and fleet-footed. Men¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tally he should be at all times perfectly cool{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and self-possessed, and of an even temper,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}always full of enthusiasm, without excite¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment. A six-footer from about 190 to 210{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pounds is the best style of a man, because{% endraw %}
{% raw %}he can better protect his quarter. Care{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should be taken not to select a fat, lazy,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phlegmatic man, because he is of abso¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lutely no use whatever.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the offensive, the first duty of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}centre-rush is to put the ball in play.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Much depends upon this. The team can{% endraw %}
{% raw %}play no faster than he does. If he is slow,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the whole thing is slow. He must fol¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}low the ball closely, and the moment it is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}down, take it from his back and put it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}down for the next play. When the ball{% endraw %}
{% raw %}goes out of bounds, he must be the first{% endraw %}
