{% raw %}POSITION PLAY{% endraw %}
{% raw %}103{% endraw %}
{% raw %}If the centre puts the ball into play by a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}toss, the quarter may stand three or four{% endraw %}
{% raw %}feet back.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The two most common ways of putting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ball into play are the "end" and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"flap snap." Take the position of quar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ter in receiving the "flap snap" first.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The quarter stands, or rather kneels with¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}out touching his knees, close up behind{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the centre about a foot from the centre.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}He should face the same flank of the line{% endraw %}
{% raw %}all the time, so as not to give the play{% endraw %}
{% raw %}away. The quarter should get his body{% endraw %}
{% raw %}low and well forward, but must not squat.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The feet should be at right angles with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the gridiron line. He should stand on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the toe of the rear foot and the ball of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forward foot, most of the weight of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}body resting on the forward foot. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rear leg should not be straight, but angu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lar. The position is such that the quar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ter can start quickly in the opposite di¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rection from that he is facing to receive{% endraw %}
