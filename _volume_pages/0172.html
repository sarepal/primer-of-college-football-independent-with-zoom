{% raw %}120 A PRIMER OF COLLEGE FOOTBALL{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The ball should be kicked as far down{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the field as possible without kicking into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}touch or kicking over the goal line. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}object is to gain as much distance as pos¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sible by the kick. If the ball is kicked{% endraw %}
{% raw %}into touch, it must be kicked over again.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}If kicked out twice, the opponent kicks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}off. There is no advantage in that. It{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is better to allow the opposing eleven to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have the ball at their ten or fifteen yard{% endraw %}
{% raw %}line than have it yourself on yours, because{% endraw %}
{% raw %}you will have to kick right off; and there{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is greater danger of a block kick from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scrimmage than from the start-off, be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cause the opponents do not stand ten{% endraw %}
{% raw %}yards back.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}If the ball be kicked over the goal line,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it will be brought out to the twenty-five-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}yard line. Better kick over, therefore,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}than to kick into touch.{% endraw %}
