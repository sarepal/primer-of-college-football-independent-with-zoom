{% raw %}181{% endraw %}
{% raw %}As to the proper style of training for an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eleven, perhaps no two persons have ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}actly the same ideas. But in general there{% endraw %}
{% raw %}may be said to be two schools : the old and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the new, or the old style and the new{% endraw %}
{% raw %}system.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The old school is one of Herculean{% endraw %}
{% raw %}labors and Spartan discipline. Its funda¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mental principle is, " Six days shalt thou{% endraw %}
{% raw %}labor." The idea of the old school is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}physical development and bodily disci¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pline. The idea of the new school seems{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to be that sport is simply a recreation, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has no other use. " Work as little as pos¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sible" seems to be the new creed. Pe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}riods of rest are emphasized rather than{% endraw %}
{% raw %}periods of work. Three days shalt thou{% endraw %}
{% raw %}labor, and then not too hard. The aim{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the new system is to train the men for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the final match, to bring them to the high¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}est physical condition by the end of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}season, which sounds rational enough.{% endraw %}
